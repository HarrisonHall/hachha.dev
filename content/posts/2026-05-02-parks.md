Last weekend, I went hiking with family in several North Carolina parks and came
to a startling realization- I have no idea which parks I've been to. It's not
hard to create a list, and physical material exists for both national and state
parks, but I wanted a single interface that would track it all and be less
ephemeral. I'm not going to trust someone else's site or app.

To cut to the chase, the site is [here](https://parks.hachha.dev/).

![park tracker](/blog/media/park_tracker.png)

While it was trivial to find datasets for national parks, I could not find a
unified dataset for combined state parks. Even by state, this was difficult. I
was able to get South Carolina park ArcGIS data, but I really wanted GPS
coordinates in addition which were nontrivial to compute from shape data.
Ultimately, I was able to leverage Wikipedia for state park data.

The site is currently fairly simple. It's a list of parks with their designation
(park, forest, etc.) and level (national, state, local, etc.). The graphical
representation leverages [leaflet.js](https://leafletjs.com) with GPS
coordinates. This is also my first time leveraging [bulma](https://bulma.io/) as
a css framework, so I'll see how I like it.

Additionally, the site supports various overrides and tracks which parks have
been visited. Eventually, I'd like to split this into 2 capabilities: a page for
park reference and a page chronologically iterating visits (i.e. a blog page).

While I do intend to open source this (and the resulting dataset) eventually, I
do not intend to make the platform so _anybody_ can track their visits. If you
want a reference for US parks, other
[resources exist](https://stateparkhq.com/).
